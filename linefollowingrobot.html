Line Following Robot

  • Max Chernotsky
  • Robotics

This robot was part of a Mechatronics Design course in my 3rd year of study. It was a group project consisting of two Mechatronics Engineering students and two Elec-Mech Engineering students. Throughout the course of the semester, we needed to design the hardware, electronics and software that would allow the robot to follow a circuit on the floor and stop before a marked area.

Initially, the chassis of the robot was decided on. The size of the components was taken into account, given the budget, weight saving and the size limitation of the final project. The chassis was designed in SolidWorks and a prototype was printed using my 3D printer. The final chassis was laser cut out of perspex and a sheet of velcro was cut and glued onto it to enable modular mounting of components.

The electronic circuits was designed afterwards. This included the line following sensors, distance sensors, microcontroller module, and the motor controller circuitry. The line sensors used infrared sensors that would detect if the line was present or not underneath the robot. The distance sensors utilised a threshold approach to distance, whereby when the robot would be within a predetermined distance from the robot, the signal would go high. The motor controller allowed us to implement PWM through the microcontroller to adjust the speed of the robot.

Finally, the software that would run on the STM32 board was coded through Eclipse. This would control the start and automation of the line following robot. Through this coding process, we were able to change the speeds and stopping distances. After some trial and error, the robot operated as designed.

Our design completed the circuit with the fastest time!

The combination between our unique design and efficient code ensures that our robot was the quickest and received the second highest mark in the class. The entire group can be seen in the image below!
